About Tokyo

Tokyo, the capital of Japan, is a bustling metropolis that offers an unforgettable experience for visitors. Known for its skyscrapers, historical landmarks, cultural festivals, and a blend of traditional and modern life, Tokyo has something to offer for everyone. Whether you're exploring ancient temples, indulging in world-class cuisine, or marveling at cutting-edge technology, you'll find endless possibilities in this vibrant city.

Top Attractions

Tokyo Skytree

The tallest structure in Japan, offering panoramic views of the entire city. You can explore the observation decks or dine in the restaurants at the top.
Learn more about Tokyo Skytree.

Senso-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest temple, located in Asakusa, attracts millions of visitors each year. The temple’s rich history and the bustling Nakamise Street nearby offer a perfect cultural experience.
Learn more about Senso-ji Temple.

Shibuya Crossing

One of the busiest intersections in the world, Shibuya Crossing symbolizes the fast-paced life of Tokyo. It’s a must-see for any visitor to experience the hustle and energy of the city.
Learn more about Shibuya Crossing.

Takeshita Street

Located in the Harajuku district of Tokyo, is a vibrant and bustling pedestrian street famous for its quirky fashion, trendy boutiques, and unique food stalls. A popular destination for youth culture and fashion enthusiasts, it offers everything from colorful accessories to eccentric clothing styles.
Learn more about the Takeshita Street.

City Information

  • Population: 13.96 million (as of 2020)
  • Geography: Located on the eastern coast of Honshu Island, Japan's largest island.
  • Language: Japanese (English widely understood in tourist areas)
  • Weather: Temperate, with hot summers and cool winters.
